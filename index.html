How to play

15 with three is a seemingly simple game, but looks can be deceiving. Your job is to roll three dice and find a way to reach 15 at the most. You can use all the basic math operators like plus, minus, divide and multiply and you can use brackets.

For example when you have the following sequence of one, two and five:

You will probably be inclined to add them up to reach eight: 1 + 2 + 5 = 8. You can, however, reach 15 if you add and multiply instead (1 + 2) * 5 = 15. It is worth-while thinking differently and not giving in to the first idea.

  • In each move you have twenty seconds to find the best result.
  • The game shows the highest possible value next to the dice.
  • If you fail to reach that you get the remainder as points, so, for example, if 15 is the highest possible value and you reach 10, you get 5 points. If you run out of time, you get the highest possible value.
  • Once you reached 20, the game is over

You can play the game by yourself or against other players. Simply add as many players as you want to and you'll play in turns. You can also remove players by clicking the x next to their name.
